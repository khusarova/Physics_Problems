This problem involves a traveling wave. To solve it, we compare the given equation to the standard form of a sinusoidal wave equation.

### 1. Standard Wave Equation
The general form for a traveling wave is:
$$y(x, t) = A \sin(kx - \omega t)$$

Where:
* **$A$** = Amplitude
* **$k$** = Wave number ($k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$)
* **$\omega$** = Angular frequency ($\omega = 2\pi f$)


From your image, the given equation is:
$$y(x, t) = 0.05 \sin(2\pi x - 50\pi t)$$

---

### 2. Step-by-Step Solution

#### **a) Amplitude $A$**
The amplitude is the coefficient in front of the sine function.
* **$A = 0.05 \text{ m}$**

#### **b) Wavelength $\lambda$**
Identify the wave number $k$ from the term attached to $x$:
$$k = 2\pi \text{ rad/m}$$
Since $k = \frac{2\pi}{\lambda}$, we can solve for $\lambda$:
$$\lambda = \frac{2\pi}{k} = \frac{2\pi}{2\pi} = 1$$
* **$\lambda = 1 \text{ m}$**

#### **c) Frequency $f$**
Identify the angular frequency $\omega$ from the term attached to $t$:
$$\omega = 50\pi \text{ rad/s}$$
Since $\omega = 2\pi f$, we can solve for $f$:
$$f = \frac{\omega}{2\pi} = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25$$
* **$f = 25 \text{ Hz}$**

#### **d) Wave speed $v$**
There are two ways to calculate this. You can use the wave relationship $v = f\lambda$ or the ratio of angular frequency to wave number $v = \frac{\omega}{k}$:
$$v = f \cdot \lambda = 25 \text{ Hz} \times 1 \text{ m} = 25$$
OR
$$v = \frac{\omega}{k} = \frac{50\pi}{2\pi} = 25$$
* **$v = 25 \text{ m/s}$**
