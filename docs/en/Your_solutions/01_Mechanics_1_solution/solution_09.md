### **Step-by-Step Solution: Momentum Comparison**

Momentum is defined as the product of an object's mass and its velocity. To compare the two, we must ensure all units are consistent (converting grams to kilograms).


---

#### **1. The Momentum Formula**
The formula for momentum ($p$) is:
$$p = m \cdot v$$
* $m$ = mass (in **kg**)
* $v$ = velocity (in **m/s**)

---

#### **2. Calculating Momentum for the Fly**
* **Mass ($m_1$):** $2 \text{ g} = 0.002 \text{ kg}$
* **Velocity ($v_1$):** $10 \text{ m/s}$

$$p_{fly} = 0.002 \cdot 10$$
$$p_{fly} = \mathbf{0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$$

---

#### **3. Calculating Momentum for the Tennis Ball**
* **Mass ($m_2$):** $60 \text{ g} = 0.06 \text{ kg}$
* **Velocity ($v_2$):** $1 \text{ m/s}$

$$p_{ball} = 0.06 \cdot 1$ $
$$p_{ball} = \mathbf{0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}}$$

---

#### **4. Comparison**
Now we compare the two results:
* Fly: $0.02 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$
* Tennis Ball: $0.06 \text{ kg}\cdot\text{m/s}$

Since $0.06 > 0.02$, the tennis ball has the greater momentum.

---

### **Final Results Summary**

| Object | Mass (kg) | Velocity (m/s) | Momentum (kg·m/s) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fly** | $0.002$ | $10$ | $0.02$ |
| **Tennis Ball** | $0.06$ | $1$ | **$0.06$** |

**Conclusion:** The **60-gram tennis ball** has greater momentum.
