### **Step-by-Step Solution: Relative Velocity**

To solve this problem, we need to use vector addition. The boat's actual path (Resultant Velocity) must point directly North. To achieve this, the boat must steer at an angle into the current (West of North) to cancel out the river's flow.

---

#### **1. Identifying the Velocity Vectors**
* **Velocity of the River ($v_r$):** $2 \text{ m/s}$ East.
* **Velocity of the Boat relative to water ($v_b$):** $5 \text{ m/s}$.
* **Width of the River:** $200 \text{ m}$.


---

#### **2. Finding the Steering Direction (Angle)**
The boat must have a horizontal velocity component that exactly cancels the river's speed ($2 \text{ m/s}$ West). 

Let $\theta$ be the angle measured from the **North direction towards the West**.
Using trigonometry in the velocity triangle:
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{\text{Opposite (River Speed)}}{\text{Hypotenuse (Boat Speed)}}$$
$$\sin(\theta) = \frac{2}{5} = 0.4$$

**Calculating the angle:**
$$\theta = \arcsin(0.4) \approx \mathbf{23.58^\circ}$$

The boat should head at an angle of **$23.58^\circ$ West of North**.

---

#### **3. Finding the Resultant Velocity ($v_{res}$)**
The speed at which the boat actually moves North is the vertical component of its velocity. We use the Pythagorean theorem:
$$v_b^2 = v_r^2 + v_{res}^2$$
$$5^2 = 2^2 + v_{res}^2$$
$$25 = 4 + v_{res}^2$$
$$v_{res} = \sqrt{21} \approx \mathbf{4.58 \text{ m/s}}$$

---

#### **4. Calculating the Time to Cross**
Since the boat is moving directly North at $4.58 \text{ m/s}$ and the river is $200 \text{ meters}$ wide:
$$\text{Time} (t) = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Velocity North}}$$
$$t = \frac{200}{\sqrt{21}} \approx \frac{200}{4.58}$$
$$t \approx \mathbf{43.64 \text{ seconds}}$$

---

### **Final Results Summary**

* **Heading Direction:** $23.58^\circ$ West of North.
* **Crossing Time:** $\approx 43.64 \text{ seconds}$.
