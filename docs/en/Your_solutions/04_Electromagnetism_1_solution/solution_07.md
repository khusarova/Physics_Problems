This problem involves two stages: first, the electron gains speed using an electric potential, and second, it enters a magnetic field where it moves in a circle.

### 1. Identify the Constants
To solve this, we need the standard physical properties of an electron:
* **Charge ($e$):** $1.6 \times 10^{-19} \text{ C}$
* **Mass ($m$):** $9.11 \times 10^{-31} \text{ kg}$

---

### 2. Find the Velocity ($v$)
The electron is accelerated from rest by a potential difference ($V = 5000 \text{ V}$). The electrical potential energy is converted into kinetic energy:
$$eV = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

Solving for velocity $v$:
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2eV}{m}}$$
$$v = \sqrt{\frac{2 \times (1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \times 5000}{9.11 \times 10^{-31}}}$$
$$v \approx 4.19 \times 10^7 \text{ m/s}$$

---

### 3. Find the Radius ($R$)
When the electron enters the magnetic field ($B = 0.1 \text{ T}$), the magnetic force acts as a centripetal force:
$$qvB = \frac{mv^2}{R}$$



Since $q = e$, we rearrange the formula to solve for the radius $R$:
$$R = \frac{mv}{eB}$$

Substitute the values:
$$R = \frac{(9.11 \times 10^{-31}) \times (4.19 \times 10^7)}{(1.6 \times 10^{-19}) \times 0.1}$$
$$R \approx 0.00238 \text{ m}$$

---

### Final Answer
The radius of the circular path is approximately **$2.38 \text{ mm}$** (or $2.38 \times 10^{-3} \text{ m}$).
